Moderation and Non-Excess

In Pranic Healing, Master Choa Kok Sui emphasizes that the path to enlightenment requires balance. Any form of excess creates congestion in the energy body and prevents spiritual progress.

Causes of Addictions & Excess

Genetic factors
Biological predisposition to addictive behaviors
Personality traits
Impulsiveness, frustration tolerance, sensitivity to rejection
Early life experiences
Formative events that shape behavior patterns
Depression / anxiety
Using excess as coping mechanism
Stress
Seeking relief through excessive behaviors
Childhood trauma
Unresolved wounds leading to compensatory behaviors

Forms of Excess

Ingestible
Drugs, alcohol, smoking, tobacco
Food in general, binge eating
Specific foods - salty, sweets, sugar, chocolates, coffee, tea
Technology
Mobile phone, gaming
Social media, reels, WhatsApp
TV, binge watching
Behavioral
Workaholism - to feel worthy
Excess meditation/spiritual focus - trying to prove myself
Sleep, rest - escape from reality

Imbalanced Virtues

Excessive loving-kindness
To others without taking care of the self
Excessive generosity
Without practicality
Excessive honesty
Without considering kindness and other factors
Excessiveness in ideas and beliefs
Leading to rigidity, prejudices, calcification
Excessive constancy
In any one area without proper balance

Generosity and Non-Stealing

Giving freely without expectation, and respecting what belongs to others.

Physical Generosity
Materially and financially
Not paying fairly
Temptation to take what's not mine
Mental Generosity
Not thinking the best of a person
Judging based on stereotypes
Copyright violation
Spiritual Generosity
Not blessing regularly those in need
Not sharing knowledge that can help
Relationship Generosity
Not being generous with time
Stealing a person's reputation
Stealing emotionally

"Generosity brings happiness at every stage of its expression.

We experience joy in forming the intention to be generous.

We experience joy in the actual act of giving something.

And we experience joy in remembering the fact that we have given."

- Dana Sutta - Lord Gautama Buddha

The Primal Wound Theory

Root Causes
Manifestations
Healing Approaches

Fundamental Causes of Disconnection

  • The illusion of non-being and creation of a false self
  • Abandonment - connected to love and heart chakra
  • Powerlessness - connected to will and solar plexus
  • Worthlessness - connected to light and crown chakra
  • Childhood experiences that reinforce these wounds

How Primal Wounds Manifest

  • "I'm not enough, I don't matter"
  • Feeling lost, alone, abandoned
  • Feeling worthless, empty, isolated
  • Feelings of terror, fear, and shame
  • Avoiding pain and vulnerability
  • Need to numb or distract oneself
  • Excessive need to control life and time

Pranic Healing Approaches

  • Energy cleansing to remove congested patterns
  • Chakra balancing to address specific wounds
  • Meditation to connect with higher self
  • Developing mindful awareness of triggers
  • Practicing moderation in all areas of life
  • Using willpower consciously through ajna chakra
  • Cultivating soul connection through crown chakra

"The spiritual path is not about extreme asceticism or indulgence, but about finding the middle way. Moderation is the key to mastering our energies and progressing on the path to enlightenment."

- Master Choa Kok Sui
